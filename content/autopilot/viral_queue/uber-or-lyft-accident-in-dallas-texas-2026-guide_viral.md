Rideshare accidents in Texas are tricky because there isn't one simple insurance policy that covers everything. Uber and Lyft use a tiered system: if the driver's app is off, it's their personal insurance. If the app is on but no ride is accepted, there's limited contingent coverage. Once a ride is accepted or a passenger is in the car, the full $1 million commercial policy kicks in. Knowing which tier applies to your crash is the first step.

Texas has a 2-year statute of limitations for personal injury claims, which sounds like plenty of time but really isn't. App data, GPS logs, and witness accounts can all disappear quickly. If you were injured as a passenger, another driver, a cyclist, or a pedestrian, you should document everything immediately — photos, screenshots of the trip, and any medical visits.
