## Reddit (helpful comment — follow sub rules)

Florida's wrongful death statute (Florida Statute § 768.16–768.26) is pretty specific about who can file and what damages are recoverable. The personal representative of the deceased's estate files the claim on behalf of surviving family members — typically a spouse, children, or parents. Each eligible survivor can pursue different types of damages, including loss of companionship, mental pain and suffering, and lost financial support.

One of the most critical things families miss is the 2-year statute of limitations from the date of death. Florida courts are strict about this. Beyond that, Miami's comparative negligence rules mean even if the deceased was partially at fault, the family may still recover — just reduced by the percentage of fault assigned.
