Underride and override truck crashes are genuinely some of the most complex accident cases to pursue legally. Unlike a typical rear-end collision, these crashes often involve federal trucking regulations, multiple liable parties (the driver, the carrier, the cargo company, sometimes the truck manufacturer), and time-sensitive evidence like the truck's electronic logging device (ELD) data and black box records. If you're in Texas, you generally have two years to file a personal injury claim, but waiting even a few weeks can cost you critical evidence.
