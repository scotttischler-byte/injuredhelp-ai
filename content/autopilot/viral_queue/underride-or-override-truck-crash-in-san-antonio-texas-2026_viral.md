Underride and override truck crashes are uniquely dangerous because of how the vehicles interact during impact. In an underride, a smaller car slides beneath the trailer of a semi-truck, often shearing off the roof. In an override, the truck rides up over a smaller vehicle. Both types cause severe structural damage that standard crash ratings don't always capture — so even a 'moderate' report can hide serious injuries.

If you were involved in one of these crashes in San Antonio, there are a few things worth knowing right away. First, preserve everything — photos, dashcam footage, medical records, and any communication from the trucking company or their insurer. Texas has specific statutes of limitations for personal injury claims, and trucking companies are required to maintain certain records that can be subpoenaed if you act quickly. An attorney can file spoliation letters to prevent evidence destruction.
