## 1. Get to safety first

Move to the shoulder if you can. Turn on hazards. Call **911** if anyone is hurt or the road is blocked.

## 2. Document the scene

Take photos of vehicles, plates, skid marks, and road conditions. Exchange names, phones, and insurance with the other driver—do not argue about fault at the scene.

## 3. Seek medical care

Adrenaline can hide injuries. Urgent care or the ER creates a medical record that may matter later.

## 4. Notify your insurer

Report the crash promptly, but avoid giving a recorded statement until you understand your rights.

## 5. Consider a free attorney consultation

If you were hurt, a licensed personal injury lawyer in your state can explain deadlines and whether you have a claim.
